    Behind the flag is a vexillological project developed in order to exhibit deeper dives into the history, geography, and vexillology of more obscure regions around the world. Instead of focusing on national flags or flags of well-known locales (England, Scotland, Quebec, etc.), Behind the Flag opts to investigate the flags of lesser-known territories and secessionist regions whose vexillological basis is not well-known. Utilizing a mix of already-present knowledge, modern relevance, and geological and cultural information, explanations for each flag are made, utilizing a mix of both informed speculation and stated fact.

    FLAG OF THE DAY

    Raetia
    ⠀
    Basic Info
    Colors
    Meanings

    Raetia was a region of the Roman Empire, located in modern-day Switzerland and Germany. Raetia largely proves an archaic region, yet it contains a soccer team competitive in the ConIFA World Cup.














    Blue:
    Raetia’s Blue Hue likely contains some representation of the various rivers present within its borders; it lies in one of Switzerland’s most river-and-lake-riddled regions. Rivers also prove a major settling point, thus proving important during the times of the Romans. Another potential explanation for Raetia’s blue tone also correlates to the natural beauty of Switzerland; with blue usually representing more peaceful, nature-based aspects of a region, blue could encompass all of the region’s pristine nature, as opposed to solely rivers.
    White:
    Raetia’s White Color is likely indicative of two possibilities: Swiss temperament or Alpine Colors. The Swiss are well-known for their neutral stance, and as Raetia as a region with a strong identity didn’t exist during the time of the Romans, it is definitely a possibility that the white color is a sign of neutrality, an inability to take sides of a color-based level (eg. red v blue). Raetia is also at the crossroads of the alpine Switzerland and industrial Germany, and given that the large proportion of the population is Swiss, the white could also represent the mountainous nature of the terrain, a more cultural representation on the flag.
    Gray:
    Raetia’s Gray Tone, as the last color on the flag, could either prove the amalgamation of the hues of the grayscaled version of the other two colors present on the flag, but likely represents the industrial side of the region. While one portion lays in Switzerland, a decently-sized section of Raetia lies in Germany, which is considered one of the more industrialized nations of the land. Thus, the Gray likely represents industry, with cobblestone and brick replacing the land in more populated areas.
